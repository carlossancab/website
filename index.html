After studying economics, I quickly moved towards becoming an applied economist with a strong interest in long-term historical perspectives. This motivated me to enroll in a Master's program in Global Economic History, ultimately leading me to complete a PhD in Economic History.

My research interests focus particularly on how preindustrial economies were organized and how key aspects of human life—such as well-being and inequality—responded to long-term transformations. More recently, I've expanded my work to cover more modern periods and topics, including the loss of human capital resulting from military conflicts, historical demography, and anthropometric history.
